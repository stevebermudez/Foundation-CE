### 3.4 Sample Unit 1 Quiz - 20 items

All 4-option multiple choice, one best answer, exam style.

1. The primary “product” that a real estate sales associate offers to the public is
   A. financing at below-market interest rates
   B. expert information on property transfer, markets, and marketing
   C. the legal right to draft deeds for customers
   D. construction management services

   **Answer:** B

2. A sales associate who specializes in listing and selling income-producing office buildings and shopping centers is most likely working in
   A. industrial sales
   B. agricultural sales
   C. residential sales
   D. commercial sales

   **Answer:** D

3. A property manager’s main responsibility is to
   A. obtain the highest possible selling price for the property
   B. protect the owner’s investment and maximize the owner’s return
   C. originate mortgage loans for tenants
   D. represent tenants in eviction proceedings

   **Answer:** B

4. Which statement regarding a comparative market analysis (CMA) is accurate
   A. A CMA must comply with USPAP and can only be prepared by a state-certified appraiser
   B. A CMA is a type of federally regulated appraisal
   C. A CMA estimates probable selling price using recent comparable sales
   D. A CMA may be described to the public as an appraisal

   **Answer:** C

5. USPAP primarily sets standards for
   A. property management agreements
   B. real estate license law enforcement
   C. ethical advertising practices
   D. appraisal practice and appraiser ethics

   **Answer:** D

6. A sales associate is asked by a customer to “do an appraisal” on the customer’s house. The sales associate prepares a detailed CMA. To comply with Florida law, the sales associate should
   A. advertise the CMA as a certified residential appraisal
   B. clearly identify the work product as a CMA, not an appraisal
   C. sign the report as a state-certified appraiser
   D. charge the same fee that a state-certified appraiser would charge

   **Answer:** B

7. Which type of residential construction involves building homes on a large scale using model homes and standardized plans
   A. Custom homes
   B. Spec homes
   C. Tract homes
   D. Modular homes

   **Answer:** C

8. A developer purchases raw land, records a subdivision plat map, installs streets and utilities, then sells improved lots to builders. This process is called
   A. assemblage
   B. subdivision and development
   C. dedication
   D. condemnation

   **Answer:** B

9. Dedication occurs when
   A. a seller dedicates a property to charity for tax benefits
   B. a government agency takes land through eminent domain
   C. a developer transfers streets or parks to a governmental body for public use
   D. a lender records a mortgage in the public records

   **Answer:** C

10. A real estate professional who specializes in analyzing existing or potential projects and providing advice to investors is primarily engaged in
    A. counseling
    B. industrial sales
    C. property management
    D. title insurance

    **Answer:** A

(You can repeat this difficulty and style up to 20 items for each unit.)

### 4.4 Sample Broker Unit Quiz - Session 1 (20 items, excerpt)

1. To qualify for a Florida broker license, the applicant must have held an active real estate license for at least
   A. 12 months during the past 3 years
   B. 24 months during the past 5 years
   C. 36 months during the past 10 years
   D. 48 months during the past 7 years

   **Answer:** B

2. Which statement best describes the consequence of failing to disclose a prior felony conviction on a broker application
   A. The application is automatically approved if the conviction is more than 7 years old
   B. The applicant must pay a higher license fee but may be licensed
   C. Failure to disclose is grounds for denial and possible disciplinary action
   D. There is no consequence if the conviction did not involve real estate

   **Answer:** C

3. The term “Florida resident” for purposes of applying under mutual recognition means an individual who has resided in Florida
   A. for at least 4 months during the previous year
   B. continuously for at least 12 months
   C. every winter season for at least 3 consecutive years
   D. for at least 30 days before submitting the application

   **Answer:** A  *(Assuming the standard 4-month rule from 475 F.S. - verify exact phrasing if you quote statute in course text.)*

4. A broker who holds multiple licenses
   A. is prohibited from operating more than one brokerage corporation
   B. may hold broker licenses in two or more brokerage entities if the Commission approves
   C. may supervise sales associates only under one of the licenses
   D. is automatically exempt from post-licensing requirements

   **Answer:** B

5. A broker’s license becomes **null and void** when
   A. the license is voluntarily surrendered or revoked by the Commission
   B. the broker has a complaint filed against them
   C. the broker changes business address
